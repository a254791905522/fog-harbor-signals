Shrouded Harbor Signals
Foggy Harbor Signal Duty
Guide ships through fog and storms at five historic lighthouses. 50 night shifts of signal duty await. Can you keep the harbor safe?
Shrouded Harbor Signals is an offline puzzle game where you stand watch as a lighthouse keeper on the Atlantic coast. Through fog, rain, thunder, and blizzards, you must read each approaching ship and reply with the correct signal before its patience runs out.

HOW TO PLAY
- Tap a ship to focus on it. Its name, type, and intent appear on screen.
- Read the ship's intent: Inbound, Outbound, Anchorage, Distress, or Unknown.
- Tap the matching signal button: Foghorn, Lamp, Flag, Ack, Warn, or SOS.
- Reply correctly before the patience timer expires. Too many mistakes ends the shift.
- Guide all required ships within the mistake limit to complete the shift.

50 NIGHT SHIFTS
Journey across five historic lighthouse stations spanning the 1930s to the 1960s:
- Portland Head (Levels 1-10, 1930s): Learn the basics with foghorn and lamp.
- Boston Harbor (Levels 11-20, 1940s): Handle heavier traffic and rain.
- Cape Cod (Levels 21-30, 1950s): Radar arrives to help spot ships in storms.
- Sandy Hook (Levels 31-40, 1950s): Thunder and low visibility test your nerve.
- Cape Ray (Levels 41-50, 1960s): Blizzards and dense traffic in the final chapter.

SIGNALS & SHIPS
- Six signal types: Foghorn, Lamp, Flag, Acknowledge, Warn, and SOS.
- Eight vessel types: Fishing, Cargo, Tanker, Passenger, Sail, Steam, Naval, and Rescue.
- Five weather conditions: Clear, Fog, Heavy Rain, Thunder, and Blizzard.
- Signals unlock progressively as you advance through each station.

FEATURES
- 50 levels across 5 historic lighthouse stations.
- Progressive difficulty: weather worsens and traffic increases.
- No score, no stars — just clear the shift with fewer mistakes.
- Fully offline. No ads, no in-app purchases, no accounts, no tracking.
- Tutorial levels (1-3) teach every signal type.
- Portrait orientation, iPhone only.

Shrouded Harbor Signals is a calm, atmospheric puzzle about reading the sea and replying in time. Every ship has a story; every shift has a challenge.
